Actionability-Shielded Fleet Dispatch for Microgrid Formation in Weak-Communication Power-Transportation-Information Networks

SAFE-GPINN studies disaster recovery in a coupled power, transportation and communication network. It converts physically feasible restoration candidates into executable actions by checking route reachability, packet evidence, controller availability and mobile-resource state before neural ranking.

Abstract

Post-disaster microgrid formation increasingly depends on mobile energy storage, electric vehicles and UAV-assisted communication. Weak communication creates an execution gap because a network-feasible action can still lack valid packets, route evidence, localization confidence or control authority. SAFE-GPINN closes this gap by applying an EWMAC actionability shield before Graph-PINN ranking and closed-loop execution. In five-seed experiments, it restores all eight released feeder faults and reaches reward 415.23 while maintaining high packet delivery under the reconstructed PTIN case.

Research object

Coupled Power, Transportation and Information Network

The object is a post-disaster PTIN restoration case. The PDN supplies loads, the UTN constrains mobile-resource travel, and the CN constrains packets, delay and controller authority. Cross-layer dependencies decide whether an apparently feasible restoration action can be executed.

PDN buses, feeders, released faults UTN junctions, roads, travel times CN nodes, links, packet feedback Fleet MESS, UGV and UAV resources

Complete modeling process

Modular Mathematical Model

Network and Task Sets

  • \(\mathcal G^P=(\mathcal V^P,\mathcal E^P)\) distribution network
  • \(\mathcal G^T=(\mathcal V^T,\mathcal E^T)\) transportation network
  • \(\mathcal G^C=(\mathcal V^C,\mathcal E^C)\) communication network
  • \(\mathcal F_t\) released and unrestored feeder faults
  • \(\mathcal M,\mathcal U,\mathcal B_t\) MESS, UAV and V2G resource sets
  • \(\mathcal D\) cross-layer dependency set

State and Decision Variables

  • \(x^P_{e,t}\in\{0,1\}\) feeder closed or restored state
  • \(z^P_{i,t}\in\{0,1\}\) PDN bus energization state
  • \(u_{m,k,t}\in\{0,1\}\) mobile resource assigned to task
  • \(q_{k,t},d_{k,t}\) packet delivery rate and delay
  • \(E^M_t,E^V_t\) MESS and V2G remaining energy
  • \(a_t\in\mathcal A_t\) selected closed-loop action

Closed-Loop State

The state records restored feeders, released latent faults, predeployed UAV targets, remaining resource energy, traffic feedback, packet feedback and AC feasibility outputs.

\[ s_t=\left(\mathcal R_t,\mathcal F_t,\mathcal U^{pre}_t,E^M_t,E^V_t,\tau^T_t,q_t,d_t,\Omega^P_t\right) \]

Candidate Action Set

The implemented closed loop considers direct restoration, UAV relay restoration, dual-channel restoration, UAV predeployment and waiting.

\[ \mathcal A_t=\mathcal A_t^{dir}\cup\mathcal A_t^{relay}\cup\mathcal A_t^{dual}\cup\mathcal A_t^{pre}\cup\{a_t^{wait}\} \]

Implemented Constraint Catalogue

These equations summarize the constraints supported by the current closed-loop code and result fields. Numerical thresholds are defined in the experiment protocol rather than embedded as unexplained constants.

Block Mathematical form Meaning
Failure release \(\mathcal F_t=\{e\in\mathcal F\mid \rho_e\le k_t,\ e\notin\mathcal R_t\}\) Only released and unrestored faults can be selected.
Task domain \(a_t^{dir},a_t^{relay},a_t^{dual}\Rightarrow k(a_t)\in\mathcal F_t,\quad a_t^{pre}\Rightarrow k(a_t)\in\mathcal F_t\cup\mathcal F^{lat}_t\) Restoration actions target released faults, while predeployment can also target latent faults that are scheduled for later release.
Power topology \(\mathcal V^{P,on}_t=\operatorname{Reach}(v_0^P,\{e\in\mathcal E^P\mid x^P_{e,t}=1\})\) Energized buses are the buses reachable from the source through closed or restored feeders.
Restoration monotonicity \(x^P_{e,t+1}\ge x^P_{e,t},\quad \mathcal R_t=\{e\in\mathcal F\mid x^P_{e,t}=1\}\) A repaired feeder remains repaired in the current closed-loop episode.
Load service \(0\le p^{srv}_{i,t}\le p^d_i z^P_{i,t},\quad e^{shed}_{t}=\Delta t_t\sum_i(p^d_i-p^{srv}_{i,t})\) Served load is bounded by demand and energization. Shed energy is accumulated over the action duration.
AC feasibility \(\underline V\le V_{i,t}\le\overline V,\quad \ell_{e,t}\le\overline \ell_e\) The AC adapter checks bus voltage and line loading after each closed-loop action.
Power gate \(\chi^P_{k,t}=\mathbf 1\{\underline V\le V^{(k)}_{i,t}\le\overline V,\ \ell^{(k)}_{e,t}\le\overline\ell_e,\ \forall i,e\}\) A candidate is power-feasible only if the post-action AC check stays within voltage and loading limits.
Mobile support \(0\le p^M_t\le \bar P^M n^M_t,\quad 0\le p^V_t\le \min(\bar P^V n^V_t,60E^V_t/\Delta t_t)\) MESS and V2G support are bounded by available units, rated power and remaining energy.
Resource energy \(E^M_{t+1}=E^M_t-p^M_t\Delta t_t/60,\quad E^V_{t+1}=E^V_t-p^V_t\Delta t_t/60\) Remaining MESS and V2G energy is updated after an applied restoration action.
Energy gate \(\chi^E_{k,t}=\mathbf 1\{E^M_t\ge e^M_{k,t},\ E^V_t\ge e^V_{k,t}\},\quad e^M_{k,t}=p^M_{k,t}\Delta t_t/60\) The action is admitted only when the remaining MESS and V2G energy can cover the projected support.
Traffic mapping \(\mathcal E^T(k)=\{r\in\mathcal E^T\mid (i,r,\mathrm{road\_lighting})\in\mathcal D,\ i\in\partial k\}\) The target road set is obtained from dependencies between PDN endpoints and UTN edges.
Travel time \(\tau^T_{k,t}=|\mathcal E^T(k)|^{-1}\sum_{r\in\mathcal E^T(k)}\tau^{SUMO}_{r,t},\quad \tilde\tau^T_{k,t}=\gamma^T\tau^T_{k,t}\) SUMO or TraCI feedback supplies route travel time, then the robust multiplier forms the conservative travel estimate.
Traffic feasibility \(\chi^T_{k,t}=\mathbf 1\{o^T_t=\mathrm{ok},\ \tilde\tau^T_{k,t}\le\bar\tau^T\}\) A task is traffic-feasible only when the traffic adapter succeeds and the robust travel time is acceptable.
Packet evidence \(q_{k,t}=N_t^{-1}\sum_{n=1}^{N_t}\mathbf 1\{\xi_{n,t}=1\},\quad d_{k,t}=N_t^{-1}\sum_{n=1}^{N_t}d_{n,t}\) Packet delivery rate and mean delay are computed from ns-3 packet evidence or the configured fallback evidence.
Communication gate \(\chi^C_{k,t}=\mathbf 1\{q_{k,t}\ge\underline q,\ d_{k,t}\le\overline d\}\) The selected action requires sufficient packet delivery and bounded communication delay.
Relay availability \(u^{relay}_{k,t}\in\{0,1\},\quad u^{relay}_{k,t}\le\mathbf 1\{|\mathcal U|>0\},\quad q^{relay}_{k,t}=g_q(q_{k,t},\tilde\tau^T_{k,t},h^{pre}_{k,t})\) UAV relay and dual-channel actions are admitted only when UAV resources exist; their communication quality is adjusted by travel burden and predeployment.
Predeployment memory \(h^{pre}_{k,t+1}=\max\{h^{pre}_{k,t},\mathbf 1(a_t=a^{pre}_k)\},\quad \delta^{relay}_{k,t}=\delta^{0,relay}(1-h^{pre}_{k,t})\) A predeployed relay target is remembered and later reduces relay coordination time.
Powered controller \(\mathcal V^{C,on}_t=\{c\mid (i,c,\mathrm{power})\in\mathcal D,\ i\in\mathcal V^{P,on}_t\}\) Communication controllers are available only when their supplying PDN buses are energized.
Controller mapping \(\mathcal C_k=\{c\in\mathcal V^C\mid (c,k,\mathrm{switch})\in\mathcal D\}\) The switch controllability relation maps each feeder task to the communication controllers that can issue its switching command.
Switch controllability \(\chi^Z_{k,t}=\mathbf 1\{\mathcal C_k=\varnothing\ \vee\ \mathcal C_k\cap\mathcal V^{C,on}_t\ne\varnothing\ \vee\ u^{relay}_{k,t}=1\}\) A feeder can be restored when no controller is required, a required controller is powered, or an admitted UAV relay provides control.
Fleet assignment \(\sum_{k\in\mathcal F_t}u_{m,k,t}\le 1,\quad \sum_{m\in\mathcal M}u_{m,k,t}\le 1\) Each mobile resource serves at most one released task, and each task receives at most one primary mobile assignment in a decision segment.
Fleet gate \(\chi^U_{k,t}=\mathbf 1\{\exists m\in\mathcal M:u_{m,k,t}=1\ \vee\ a_t\in\mathcal A_t^{pre}\cup\{a_t^{wait}\}\}\) Restoration requires an available mobile assignment unless the action is a predeployment or wait action.
Action duration \(\Delta t_t(a)=\Delta t^0+\tilde\tau^T_{k,t}/60+\delta^{relay}_{k,t}+\delta^{retry}_{k,t}\) Execution time combines the base decision duration, robust travel time, relay coordination and retry time when communication is unavailable.
Executable action set \(\mathcal A^{safe}_t=\{a\in\mathcal A_t\mid \chi^P_{a,t}\chi^T_{a,t}\chi^C_{a,t}\chi^Z_{a,t}\chi^E_{a,t}\chi^U_{a,t}=1\}\) An action is executable only when power, traffic, communication, controller, energy and fleet gates are all satisfied.
Action application \(y_{a,t}=\mathbf 1\{a_t\in\mathcal A^{safe}_t\},\quad b_{a,t}=1-y_{a,t}\) The environment applies an admitted action and records a blocked attempt when any gate fails.
Restoration transition \(\mathcal R_{t+1}=\mathcal R_t\cup\{k(a_t)\mid y_{a,t}=1,\ a_t\in\mathcal A_t^{dir}\cup\mathcal A_t^{relay}\cup\mathcal A_t^{dual}\}\) A feeder is added to the restored set only after an admitted restoration action is applied.
Terminal condition \(g_t=\mathbf 1\{\mathcal F_t=\varnothing\},\quad T_{end}=\min\{t\mid g_t=1\}\) The episode reaches full restoration when all released faults are restored; terminal time is reported as a primary metric.

Total Objective

The implemented reward maximizes weighted restored load and terminal restoration while penalizing shed energy, action duration, packet loss, traffic delay, communication delay, unavailable control, blocked actions and AC violations.

\[ \begin{aligned} \max_{\pi}\quad &\mathbb E_{\pi}\sum_{t\in\mathcal T} \Big[ \alpha_L W_{a_t} -\alpha_E e^{shed}_t -\alpha_T\Delta t_t -\alpha_Q(1-q_{a_t,t})\\ &-\alpha_\tau[\tau^T_t-\bar\tau^0]_+ -\alpha_D[d_{a_t,t}-\bar d]_+ -\alpha_B b_{a_t,t} -\alpha_C(1-\chi^C_{a_t,t}\chi^Z_{a_t,t})\\ &-\alpha_V[\underline V-\min_i V_{i,t}]_+ -\alpha_S[\max_e \ell_{e,t}-\bar \ell]_+ +\alpha_F\mathbf 1\{\mathcal F_t=\varnothing\} \Big]. \end{aligned} \]

SAFE-GPINN details

Algorithm Modules

1

Candidate generation builds restore, relay, predeployment and waiting actions from released faults, mobile fleet locations and current PTIN state.

2

EWMAC admission tests power, traffic, communication, controller, memory and fleet gates before any neural ranking is allowed.

3

Graph-PINN residual ranking estimates physical margin, load recovery gain and cross-layer action risk over the admitted set.

4

Pairwise order regularization and prefix feasibility keep high-score actions consistent with executable restoration order.

5

Closed-loop execution updates PTIN state, records blocked reasons and triggers relay predeployment when communication is the bottleneck.

Action Selection Rule

\[ a_t^\star=\arg\max_{a\in\mathcal A_t^{\mathrm{safe}}} \left[ S_\pi(s_t,a)+\lambda_R Z_\phi(s_t,a)+\lambda_K\kappa_t(a) \right]. \]

Data and simulator design

Layered Data Support for PTIN Experiments

Reconstructed PTIN Case

PDN, UTN and CN nodes and edges are stored as auditable tables. The case also records cross-layer dependencies, failure-release steps and mobile fleet resources.

Traffic and SUMO

The UTN graph provides the route substrate. SUMO or SUMO-derived TraCI feedback supplies travel time, speed, waiting time and occupancy, which determine action duration and traffic feasibility.

Communication and ns-3

CN topology and packet replay are converted into packet delivery rate, delay and powered-controller rate. UAV relay and dual-channel actions are evaluated through these quantities.
